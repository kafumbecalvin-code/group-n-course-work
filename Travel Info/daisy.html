Plan Your Journey

Everything you need to know for a smooth and memorable trip to Uganda

Getting There

Your gateway to the Pearl of Africa

By Air

Main Airport: Entebbe International Airport (EBB)

Located 40km from Kampala, with direct flights from major cities including:

  • Dubai, Doha, Istanbul
  • Nairobi, Addis Ababa
  • Amsterdam, Brussels
  • Johannesburg

Flight Time: 8-12 hours from Europe, 4-6 hours from Middle East

By Road

Cross-border connections from neighboring countries:

  • Kenya: Via Busia or Malaba (8-10 hours from Nairobi)
  • Rwanda: Via Katuna or Cyanika (4-5 hours from Kigali)
  • Tanzania: Via Mutukula (12-14 hours from Dar es Salaam)

Modern buses and shared taxis available at all major crossings

Visa Requirements

E-Visa Available: Apply online before travel

  • Tourist Visa: $50 USD (single entry, 90 days)
  • East Africa Visa: $100 USD (Kenya, Rwanda, Uganda)
  • Visa on arrival available for most nationalities
  • Passport valid for 6+ months required

Website: visas.immigration.go.ug

Best Time to Visit

Uganda's tropical climate offers year-round travel opportunities

Dry Seasons (Best for Safari & Hiking)

June - September

Peak Season: Ideal for wildlife viewing and gorilla trekking

  • Less rainfall, easier road access
  • Animals gather at water sources
  • Perfect for Murchison Falls and Queen Elizabeth parks
  • Book accommodations early
December - February

Short Dry Season: Great weather, fewer crowds

  • Warm and sunny
  • Excellent for bird watching
  • Good for mountain gorilla tracking

Wet Seasons (Low Season)

March - May

Long Rains: Lush landscapes, fewer tourists

  • Lower accommodation prices
  • Beautiful green scenery
  • Excellent for bird watching (migratory birds)
  • Some roads may be challenging
October - November

Short Rains: Intermittent showers

  • Budget-friendly rates
  • Waterfalls at their most spectacular
  • Less crowded national parks
Pro Tip: Uganda's equatorial location means temperatures remain fairly constant year-round (20-27°C). Pack layers for cooler evenings and higher altitudes!

Accommodation Options

From budget hostels to luxury lodges - find your perfect stay

Luxury Lodges & Resorts

Price Range: $200 - $800+ per night

High-end safari lodges, eco-resorts with stunning views, and five-star hotels in Kampala. Perfect for honeymoons and special occasions.

Examples: Bwindi Lodge, Sanctuary Gorilla Forest Camp, Chobe Safari Lodge

Mid-Range Hotels & Guesthouses

Price Range: $50 - $150 per night

Comfortable hotels with good amenities, located in cities and near national parks. Great value for money with breakfast included.

Examples: Lake Heights Hotel, Entebbe Airport Guest House

Budget Hostels & Campsites

Price Range: $10 - $40 per night

Backpacker-friendly hostels, dormitories, and campsites. Social atmosphere perfect for solo travelers and groups.

Examples: Red Chilli Hideaway, Bushpig Backpackers

Safari Camps & Bandas

Price Range: $30 - $120 per night

Traditional safari tents and bandas (wooden huts) inside or near national parks. Authentic bush experience with basic to moderate comfort.

Examples: UWA Bandas in Murchison Falls, Buhoma Community Camp

Homestays & Community Tourism

Price Range: $20 - $60 per night

Stay with local families for a cultural immersion experience. Home-cooked meals and authentic interactions included.

Benefits: Support local communities, cultural exchange

Booking Tips
  • Book 3-6 months ahead for peak season
  • Gorilla trekking lodges fill up quickly
  • Many lodges include meals and activities
  • Ask about airport transfers
  • Check for malaria-free accommodation at higher altitudes

Safety & Travel Etiquette

Stay safe and respectful during your visit

Safety Tips

Health Precautions: Yellow fever vaccination required. Malaria prophylaxis recommended. Drink bottled water only.
Money Safety: Use ATMs in banks or malls. Keep cash in money belt. Credit cards accepted in major hotels and restaurants.
Transportation: Use registered taxis or ride apps (Uber, Bolt, SafeBoda). Avoid traveling alone at night. Lock car doors in cities.
Emergency Contacts: Police 999 | Ambulance 911 | Tourist Police 0800-100-200
Documents: Keep copies of passport and visa. Register with your embassy. Carry identification always.

Cultural Etiquette

Greetings: Handshakes are common. Use right hand only. Say "Hello" or local greeting "Oli otya?" (How are you?)
Photography: Always ask permission before photographing people. Don't photograph military installations or government buildings.
Dress Code: Dress modestly, especially in rural areas. Cover shoulders and knees. Light, breathable fabrics recommended.
Social Norms: Public displays of affection are frowned upon. Use both hands when giving/receiving items. Remove shoes when entering homes.
Wildlife Respect: Follow park ranger instructions. Keep 7m distance from gorillas. No flash photography with animals. Don't litter.
Important: Uganda is a conservative country. Be respectful of local customs and traditions. Learn a few words in Luganda or local languages - locals appreciate the effort!
