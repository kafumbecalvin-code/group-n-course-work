Top Attractions

Murchison Falls National Park

Where the Nile squeezes through a 7-meter gap and explodes into the world's most powerful waterfall.” Uganda's largest park, home to lions, elephants, giraffes, and unforgettable boat cruises to the thunderous falls.

Bwindi impenatrable

Home of the gentle giants. A mystical rainforest famous for mountain gorilla trekking, rich birdlife, and serene nature trails wrapped in cool mist.

Queen Elizabeth National Park

East Africa's adventure capital. From white-water rafting and bungee jumping to calm boat rides at the Source of the Nile, this is Uganda’s adrenaline home.

Kampala City Tour

The city that never stops moving. Uganda's vibrant capital blends culture, nightlife, history, and modern life. Explore bustling markets, visit the Uganda Museum, tour the iconic Kasubi Tombs, enjoy sunset views at Lake Victoria, and experience Kampala’s famous warm hospitality.

Jinja - Source of the Nile

East Africa's adventure capital. From white-water rafting and bungee jumping to calm boat rides at the Source of the Nile, this is Uganda's adrenaline home.

The Equator

Stand in two hemispheres at once. A must-stop landmark for photos, cool Coriolis demonstrations, and craft shops along the Masaka-Mbarara road.

Activities to watch out for

Boat cruise on the Source of the Nile

Sail where the Nile begins its journey. Relax, spot hippos and birds, and enjoy breathtaking views along the world's longest river.

Gorilla Trekking

Meet the gentle giants. Step into the misty forest and come face-to-face with mountain gorillas — a once-in-a-lifetime experience.

Cultural tours

Step into Uganda's heart. Discover vibrant traditions, music, dance, and crafts while connecting with friendly local communities.

Bungee Jumping

Leap into adventure! Feel the adrenaline as you plunge over the Nile, East Africa's ultimate thrill-seeker experience.

Game drives

Safari like never before. Spot lions, elephants, giraffes, and zebras in Uganda's wild parks while enjoying guided wildlife adventures.

Travel information

Best time to visit: June-September, December-February.

Visa requirements: Most nationalities require a visa to enter Uganda. Check with your local Ugandan embassy.

Climate : Warm but varies by region.
